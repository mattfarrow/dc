My work

I'm an independent carpenter and builder working throughout Surrey, West Sussex and Hampshire on a wide range of projects.

I like to try and minimise the environmental impact of any project I undertake.

Traditional green oak framing

You don't have to embark on a "grand design" to enjoy the aesthetics of oak. This ages old method of building can lend beauty to any structure - the photographs here include an oak-framed garage frontage and a log store.

Roundwood timber construction

Using timber "in the round", unprocessed and untreated, helps to preserve the natural contours and beauty of the tree. The choice of wood informs the design of a structure from the outset - making the most of the individual aesthetic and physical properties of the material. This type of construction is great for outdoor, play and garden structures, or anywhere a more rustic and natural feel is preferred.

In some of the photographs on this site you can see an outdoor classroom/play-structure that I built for Barnkids, a children's nursery. For this project I sourced local sweet chestnut poles for the frame and cedar shingles for the roof.

Conventional carpentry and building

Kitchen fitting, hanging doors, laying oak floors, building decks - you name it, I do it; from minor alterations and remodelling projects to extensions, conversions and full refurbishments. Any unusual or 'out of the ordinary' ideas more than welcome!

Advice on low-impact and environmentally friendly construction

Many of us dream of our very own eco Grand Design, but in reality most of us live in conventional houses. But that doesn't mean you can't reduce your home's environmental impact.

I can advise on simple measures you can take that will make a difference - from getting your insulation right to sourcing certified timber products, or minimising the impact of a conventional extension by making the right decisions at the design stage.
